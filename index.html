Visa Stuff
Isn't it hard to get a work visa in the US?

Yes. But within my student visa category I am allowed to work for a maximum of 1 year, and because I am a STEM major I can apply for a 2 year extension meaning a possible total of 3 years before needing to apply for an official work visa. But as I will taking up Singaporean citizenship I will no longer be eligible for a student visa and therefore this work allowance.

The US work visa application works on a lottery system, and it involves needing an employer to sponsor and vouch for you. It's a little bit easier for Singaporean citizens, so that should work in my favour should I decide to go back to the US.

Okay, but what's STEM?

But how does your course fit into STEM?

The extension only applies to certain majors within STEM, and there is a published list of degrees that are applicable. Graphic design and web development both fall under that.
